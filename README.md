# Design and Fabrication of an LPG Refrigeration System

## 1) Project Overview
This bachelor's thesis focuses on the design, fabrication, and experimental evaluation of an LPG-powered refrigeration system as an alternative to a conventional domestic refrigerator. The project investigates the feasibility of using Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) as an environmentally friendly refrigeration technology without electrical power.
The system was designed, fabricated, assembled, and experimentally tested to compare its cooling performance, efficiency, and operating characteristics with those of a domestic vapour-compression refrigerator.

## 2) Objectives
The objectives of this project were to:
- Design and fabricate an LPG refrigeration system.
- Investigate the refrigeration effect produced by LPG expansion.
- Evaluate the cooling performance of the developed system.
- Compare the LPG refrigeration system with a conventional domestic refrigerator.
- Assess the technical feasibility and potential energy savings of LPG refrigeration technology.

## 4) Methodology
The project was carried out through the following stages:
1. Literature review of LPG refrigeration technology.
2. Mechanical design of the refrigeration system.
3. Selection of components and materials.
4. Fabrication and assembly of the prototype.
5. Experimental testing under different operating conditions.
6. Measurement of cooling performance.
7. Performance comparison with a domestic refrigerator.
8. Analysis and interpretation of experimental results.
